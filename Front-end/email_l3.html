Email Etiquette for the Workplace

Here are some best practices for email communication in the workplace:

Use a clear subject line: Include a call to action (e.g., “[Action Required] 30-minute meeting”).

Stick to one thread for a topic: Keep all related emails in the same chain for clarity.

Manage recipients carefully: Always explain why you are adding or removing someone from an email chain (e.g., “+Meagan (HR) - Mike (Admin)”).

Summarize key points in replies: Recap previous messages to ensure everyone’s on the same page.

Use hyperlinks for links: Make your emails look clean by adding links instead of pasting full URLs.

Change default reply settings: Set your email to reply to only the sender, not “Reply All,” to avoid confusion.

Set undo send to 30 seconds: This gives you time to correct mistakes after hitting “Send.”
