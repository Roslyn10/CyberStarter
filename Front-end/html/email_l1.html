Lesson 1: Email Safety Tips

Email is a big part of everyday communication, whether you’re a student, employee, or just sending messages to friends and family. Here are some key tips to stay safe when using email:

For Students:

  • Keep your passwords safe: Never share your email passwords with anyone.
  • Watch out for phishing attempts: Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into sharing personal information, like your password.
  • Use strong passwords or passphrases: Make sure your passwords are hard to guess.
  • Be careful with public Wi-Fi: Don’t send personal info over public networks without protection (like a VPN).
  • Back up your data regularly: Save important files in case something happens to your account.
  • Be cautious of email attachments and links: Don’t click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  • Update your software regularly: This keeps your account and computer safer from threats.

For Employees:

  • Use strong passwords and change them often: This keeps your work information secure.
  • Watch out for phishing emails: Be cautious about emails asking for sensitive work information.
  • Be careful with email recipients: Make sure to send sensitive information only to the right people.
  • Don’t open suspicious attachments: If you don’t know who sent the email, don’t open any files attached.
  • Keep email threads organized: Stick to one email thread for one topic to avoid confusion.

For Kids:

  • Never share your password: Keep them secret, even from your friends.
  • Ask an adult if you get strange emails: Don’t open emails from people you don’t know.
  • Be careful about sharing information: Don’t share your address, phone number, or personal details through email.
