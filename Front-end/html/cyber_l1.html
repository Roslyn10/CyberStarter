Lesson 1: Cyberbullying and Online Harassment

What is Cyberbullying? Cyberbullying occurs when individuals use the internet, social media, or messaging platforms to harass, humiliate, or threaten others. This includes actions like sending mean messages, posting humiliating or fake images, sharing private information, or making threats. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen 24/7 and reach a wide audience quickly, making it even more harmful.

Examples of Cyberbullying:

  • Sending or sharing cruel or threatening messages via text, social media, or email.
  • Posting or sharing embarrassing photos or videos of someone without their consent.
  • Spreading rumors, lies, or gossip online to damage someoneâ€™s reputation.
  • Impersonating someone to send harmful messages on their behalf.
  • Excluding someone from online groups or conversations.
