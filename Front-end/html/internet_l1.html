Lesson 1: Internet Safety

Now that we understand the basics of the internet, it’s important to learn how to stay safe while using it. Here are some key tips:

1. Protect Your Personal Information

  • Don’t share sensitive details online like your name, address, phone number, or passwords.
  • Avoid meeting strangers you only know online. It’s safer to communicate only with people you know personally, like friends and family.
  • Be cautious about accepting files from unknown sources — they could contain viruses or harmful content that could cause your information to be breached.

2. Use Privacy Safeguards

  • Always turn on privacy settings on your accounts and devices to protect your personal information.
  • Always log out/sign out when using shared or public devices (e.g., school or library computers).

3. Be Smart When Browsing

  • Avoid suspicious sites or explicit content. If something looks unsafe and suspicious, it probably is.
  • Download apps carefully from trusted sources, as malware can be disguised as legitimate software and can cause issues with your devices.

4. Secure Your Internet Connection

  • Use secure networks, especially when accessing private information.
  • When using public Wi-Fi, always be cautious about information used; use a VPN for extra safety.

5. Use Strong Passwords

  • Create complex passwords with at least 16 characters, including numbers and special symbols.
  • Avoid reusing the same password across multiple sites.
  • Consider using a password manager to safely store and manage your passwords.

6. Shop Online Safely

  • Check for secure websites before entering payment details. Look for URLs that start with https instead of http.

7. Think Before You Post

  • Be mindful of what you share online, as it can stay there permanently.
  • Don’t post anything you wouldn’t want your parents, teachers, or employers to see.
  • Never share sensitive pictures or documents publicly.

8. Socialize with Care

  • Don’t trust strangers online. Cybercriminals can create fake profiles to steal personal information.
  • It’s advised not to take random people online at face value.

9. Stay Updated and Protected

  • Keep antivirus software up to date to protect against new threats, as cybercriminals are constantly learning how to bypass antivirus software.
  • Regularly back up your data to ensure that you can still have access to it after a breach.

10. Be Cautious of Links

  • Don’t click suspicious links, as they may lead to malicious websites or install malware or spyware.

Common Online Threats

Be aware of:

  • Identity theft
  • Data breaches
  • Malware and viruses
  • Phishing and scam emails
  • Fake websites and online scams
  • Romance scams
  • Inappropriate content
  • Cyberbullying
  • Poor privacy settings
