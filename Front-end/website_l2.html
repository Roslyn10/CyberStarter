Lesson 2: How to Recognize Legitimate vs. Harmful Websites

While surfing the internet, it is important to understand the difference between a legitimate (safe) and harmful (fake or fraudulent) website.

Secure websites typically use HTTPS (HyperText Transfer Protocol Secure) in their URLs, which means that the connection between the website and your device is encrypted and safer for sharing sensitive information.

  • Look for a padlock icon near the URL in your browser. Clicking this icon displays connection details and confirms if the website is secure.
  • Unsecure websites will have HTTP instead of HTTPS, and your browser may flag them as “Not Secure.”

Additionally, harmful websites often have suspicious characteristics:

Misspelled URLs or unusual domain names that don't match the brand can be a red flag.

  • Unprofessional layout or filled with pop-ups and poor spelling and grammar
  • No “Contact page” or “About Us” information.
  • Suspicious or fake-looking user reviews.
